Master and Slave Motion Overview

Use master and slave motion to synchronize one or more slave axes to a master axis. The motion of the slave axes is a function of the motion of the master axis.

The table that follows shows the different types of master and slave motion from the most basic to the most complex.

Table:     Types of Master and Slave Motion

Master and Slave Motion Type Description

Camming

The position or velocity of a slave axis depends on the position of the master axis and a cam table.

Gearing

The velocity of a slave axis is a fixed ratio of the velocity of the master axis.

Handwheel

The position of a slave axis is synchronized to an encoder signal, such as an auxiliary encoder of the master axis connected to a handwheel.

Master and slave motion does not apply the acceleration limiting feature, the feedrate limiting feature, or the automatic acceleration feature.

These conditions automatically terminate the relationship between the slave axis and the master axis:

  • If you use the ABORT Command on the master axis or to the slave axis.
  • If you disable the slave axis.
  • If software clamping occurs on the slave axis.
  • If the controller generates an axis fault on the slave axis and that fault is set in the FaultMaskDisable Parameter or in the FaultMaskDecel Parameter.
  • If you do stepper verification on the slave axis.
