ENCODER OUT ON/OFF Command

Configures an Npaq, XC4, XC4e, or XR3 to echo incoming encoder signals out of the applicable port.

Syntax:

 
ENCODER OUT <Axis> ON <Channel1>
 
ENCODER OUT <Axis> ON <Channel1>, <Channel2>
 
ENCODER OUT <Axis> ON <Channel1>, <Channel2>, <Channel3>
 
ENCODER OUT <Axis> OFF

Example:

 
ENCODER OUT X ON 2, 0, 8
ENCODER OUT X OFF

Table:     ENCODER OUT ON/OFF Command Arguments

Argument

Type

Description

<Axis>

Axis

The axis on which to execute the command.
On the Npaq, the command must be executed on the first axis.
On the XC4, XC4e, and XR3, the command must be executed on the axis for which encoder signals are being echoed.

<Channel1>

32-bit signed integer

(Optional) The incoming encoder channel to be echoed out of the first high-speed output on the Npaq or XR3, or out of the SYNC A port on the XC4 or XC4e. To see the possible values, refer to Table: Channel Argument Values.

<Channel2>

32-bit signed integer

(Optional) The incoming encoder channel to be echoed out of the second high-speed output on the Npaq, or out of the SYNC B port on the XC4 or XC4e. This argument applies only to the Npaq, XC4 and XC4e. To see the possible values, refer to Table: Channel Argument Values.

<Channel3>

32-bit signed integer

(Optional) The incoming encoder channel to be echoed out of the third high-speed output on the Npaq. This argument applies only to the Npaq. To see the possible values, refer to Table: Channel Argument Values.

Hardware: This command applies only when used with the Npaq, XC4, XC4e, or XR3. It does not apply to the Npaq MR.

ENCODER OUT ON

Use the ENCODER OUT ON command to configure and enable echoing of encoder inputs out of the applicable port on the Npaq, XC4, XC4e, or XR3. This command uses the <Channel> argument value to set the incoming encoder signal source that is echoed out of the corresponding high-speed output channel.

Warning: When you use this command to configure the functions of the Sync ports on the XC4 or XC4e or of the High-Speed Outputs on the XR3, the new functions of the outputs override all existing functions that you previously configured with other commands.

Example: Npaq

' For an Npaq with the first Axis X, send the input from J2 to J8 outputs 8 and 9.
' Do not send an input to the J8 outputs 10 and 11.
' Send the input from Channel 8 of the MXR or Resolver to the J8 outputs 12 and 13.
ENCODER OUT X ON 2, 0, 8.

Example: XC4 or XC4e

' For XC4 or XC4e Axis Y, send the input from AUX I/O to the SYNC A port.
ENCODER OUT Y ON 1

Example: XR3

' For XR3 Axis Y, send the input from AUX I/O to HOUT Y-axis outputs 0 and 1.
ENCODER OUT Y ON 1

ENCODER OUT OFF

Use the ENCODER OUT OFF command to disable echoing of encoder inputs out of the applicable port on the Npaq, XC4, XC4e, or XR3. Then the outputs can be used for other operations.

Example

' For Axis X, disable echoing of encoder signals out of the high-speed outputs.
ENCODER OUT X OFF
