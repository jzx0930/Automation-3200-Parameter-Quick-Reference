Specifying I/O Numbers

You can specify analog and digital inputs and outputs to single-axis and multi-axis drives.

For Single-Axis Drives

Analog I/O Commands

You must specify all of the analog I/O commands to the axis of the controller.

Digital I/O Commands

You must specify all of the digital I/O commands to the axis of the controller.

For Multi-Axis Drives

Analog I/O Commands

You must specify all of the analog I/O commands to the correct axes. Each axis gives you access to a maximum of two analog inputs and two analog outputs:

  • The first axis gives you access to the first and second analog input and the first and second analog output. Each group of inputs and outputs is indexed from 0 - 1.
  • The second axis gives you access to the third and fourth analog input and the third and fourth analog output. Each group of inputs and outputs is indexed from 0 - 1.

The axes use this sequence to supply access to the remaining analog inputs and outputs on the controller. If you have less than two inputs or outputs per axis and you specify the analog I/O commands to an axis that is not within the range of the inputs and outputs, the controller ignores these commands.

For Example

You have a controller with two analog inputs and two analog outputs. You issue the analog I/O commands to the second axis. The controller ignores these commands because the second axis gives you access to the third and fourth analog inputs and outputs. The controller will use these analog I/O commands only if they are issued to the first axis, which gives you access to the first and second analog inputs and outputs.

To find the number of analog inputs and outputs that are available on your controller, refer to the Physical I/O section of I/O Overview.

Digital I/O Commands

You must specify all of the digital I/O commands to the correct axes. Each axis gives you access to a maximum of 32 digital inputs and 32 digital outputs:

  • The first axis gives you access to the first 32 digital inputs and the first 32 digital outputs. Each group of inputs and outputs is indexed from 0 - 31.
  • The second axis gives you access to the second 32 digital inputs and the second 32 digital outputs. Each group of inputs and outputs is indexed from 0 - 31.

If you have less than 32 inputs or outputs per axis and you specify the digital I/O commands to an axis that is not within the range of the inputs and outputs, the controller ignores these commands.

For Example

You have a controller with 16 digital inputs and outputs. You issue the digital I/O commands to the second axis. The controller ignores these commands because the second axis gives you access to the second 32 digital inputs and outputs. The controller will use these digital I/O commands only if they are issued to the first axis, which gives you access to the first 32 digital inputs and outputs.

To find the number of digital inputs and outputs that are available on your controller, refer to the Physical I/O section of I/O Overview.
