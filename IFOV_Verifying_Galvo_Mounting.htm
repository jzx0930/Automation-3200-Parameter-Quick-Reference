IFOV: Verifying the Galvo Mounting

When the programming direction is correct, the direction is the same on the galvo axes and the servo axes. To verify that the direction is correct and that the CountsPerUnit Parameter is set to the correct value on the galvo axes, use the procedure that follows. The procedure uses these axis configurations:

  • X and Y are servo axes.
  • A and B are galvo axes.
  • X and A move in the same direction.
  • Y and B move in the same direction.

TO VERIFY THE GALVO MOUNTING

WARNING: Refer to your laser manual and obey all of the safety precautions for the laser. To prevent injury and equipment damage, make sure that the laser output signals are configured correctly before you do an operation that causes the laser to fire.
  1. Connect a laser pointer to the galvo scan head.
  2. Open Motion Composer.
  3. Power on the laser. Do one of the steps that follow:
    • Enable the guide laser.
    • Use the GALVO LASEROVERRIDE ON Command to enable the laser.
  4. Jog the X servo axis in the positive direction and the A galvo axis in the positive direction. Based on the results, do one of the steps that follow:
    • If the two axes move in the same direction, the parameter configuration is correct. Continue to Step 5.
    • If the two axes move in opposite directions, change the value of the ReverseMotionDirection Parameter on the axis that moves in the incorrect direction.
  5. Jog the Y servo axis in the positive direction and the B galvo axis in the positive direction. Based on the results, do one of the steps that follow:
    • If the two axes move in the same direction, the parameter configuration is correct. Continue to Step 6.
    • If the two axes move in opposite directions, change the value of the ReverseMotionDirection Parameter on the axis that moves in the incorrect direction.
  6. Verify that the CountsPerUnit Parameter is set correctly for the A and B galvo axes. Do the steps that follow:
    1. Draw a small part that includes only these two galvo axes.
    2. Measure the part to make sure that the dimensions are correct.
