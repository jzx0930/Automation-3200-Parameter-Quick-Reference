Connecting the System

A standard, integrated Aerotech system includes these items.

  • A3200
  • A standard or custom Aerotech motor
  • A standard or custom Aerotech mechanical stage
  • Aerotech interconnect cables to connect your hardware to the A3200
  • Aerotech interconnect drawing

Before you connect your system, make sure that you know the correct communication connections. In the table that follows, refer to the section for your drive.

If you purchased integration from Aerotech, use this procedure to connect your system. Aerotech uses the sales order number for all configuration information, which includes system wiring drawings, parameter files, and other system-specific files.

To connect an Integrated System
  1. Use a FireWire or HyperWire cable to connect a drive to the PC. If you want to connect only one drive, connect the drive and continue to Step 3.
  2. Aerotech configured the channel switches at the factory. The drive is labeled with the sales order number, axis name, and communication channel number.
  3. If you want to connect the drive to other drives, connect the drives to each other through FireWire or HyperWire cables to create a chain of drives.
  4. Do the following for the applicable drive types.
    • Non-piezo drives Connect a motor or stage to each non-piezo drive.
    • Piezo drives Connect a piezo stage to each piezo drive.
  5. Your integrated system includes interconnect cables and an interconnect drawing. The drawing is an electronic copy of the system specifications and the system interconnect. It is included on the installation device. Refer to the interconnect drawing and use the interconnect cables to connect the motors and stages to the drives.
  6. Connect the motor bus and auxiliary power.
  7. The power must be connected last and must be connected by approved personnel. Most drives have separate power connections for motor bus power and logic power so that the motor power can be interrupted by an external safety circuit (emergency stop) without losing communication with the drive module. The Npaq has optional internal circuitry to disconnect motor bus power from the main power source. For more information, refer to the hardware manuals for your drives.

If you did not purchase integration, or if you are using third-party drives, motors, or stages, use the applicable procedure to connect your system.

To Connect an Independent System with FireWire
  1. Use a FireWire cable to connect a drive to the PC. If you want to connect only one drive, connect the drive and continue to Step 3.

    Use the Device Number switches on your drive to assign the communication channel number on the FireWire network. Each drive must have a unique communication channel. The Npaq, Nstep, and Nservo must have a unique range of communication channels. To configure more than one drive, use sequential communication channels. Refer to the Communication Channel Settings section of the hardware manual for your drive.

  2. If you want to connect the drive to other drives, connect the drives to each other through FireWire cables to create a chain of drives.
  3. Do the following for the applicable drive types.
    • Non-piezo drives Connect a motor or stage to each non-piezo drive.
    • Piezo drives Connect a piezo stage to each piezo drive.
  4. Custom wiring is necessary to connect the motors and stages to the drives. To make your own interconnect cables, refer to the hardware manuals for your motors and stages. Also refer to the hardware manuals for your drives for information about the pinouts of the connectors and wiring a motor.
  5. Connect the motor bus and auxiliary power.
  6. The power must be connected last and must be connected by approved personnel. Most drives have separate power connections for motor bus power and logic power so that the motor power can be interrupted by an external safety circuit (emergency stop) without losing communication with the drive module. The Npaq has optional internal circuitry to disconnect motor bus power from the main power source. For more information, refer to the hardware manuals for your drives.
To Connect an Independent System with HyperWire
  1. Use a HyperWire cable to connect a drive to the PC. If you want to connect only one drive, connect the drive and continue to Step 3.
  2. If you want to connect the drive to other drives, connect the drives to each other through HyperWire cables to create a chain of drives.
  3. Do the following for the applicable drive types.
    • Non-piezo drives Connect a motor or stage to each non-piezo drive.
    • Piezo drives Connect a piezo stage to each piezo drive.
  4. Custom wiring is necessary to connect the motors and stages to the drives. To make your own interconnect cables, refer to the hardware manuals for your motors and stages. Also refer to the hardware manuals for your drives for information about the pinouts of the connectors and wiring a motor.
  5. Connect the motor bus and auxiliary power.
  6. The power must be connected last and must be connected by approved personnel. Most drives have separate power connections for motor bus power and logic power so that the motor power can be interrupted by an external safety circuit (emergency stop) without losing communication with the drive module. For more information, refer to the hardware manuals for your drives.
  7. Assign a unique communication channel number to each drive on the HyperWire network. Refer to Assigning Drive Communication Channels for more information.

Hardware Manuals

The hardware manuals supply information about the installation, configuration, electrical connections, and specifications of your drive. The manuals are on your software installation device and are installed with the software. If you accepted the default location when you installed the software, the manuals are in C:\Program Files\Aerotech\A3200\Manuals. You can also download your manual from the Manuals, Help Files, & Cable Drawings section of www.aerotech.com.

To see a list of available hardware manuals, refer to Other Documentation.
