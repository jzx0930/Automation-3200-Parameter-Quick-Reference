Scanning and Marking

This topic tells you about the commands that do scanning and marking operations for a GL4 or an Nmark CLS, GCL, or SSaM. You must read and understand this topic to correctly program the GL4 or the Nmark drive.

The A3200 is a multi-axis controller that can move more than one axis at a time. Unlike most A3200 drives, which typically control mechanical axes of motors and stages, the GL4 and the Nmark CLS, GCL, and SSaM control small, high-bandwidth mirrors inside a galvo scanner.

Marking Motion

Marking motion refers to moving any of the axes controlled by the GL4 or the Nmark CLS, GCL, or SSaM while the laser output of the drive is activated. Marking motion occurs when you issue a LINEAR, CW, CCW, or BEZIER command on any GL4 or Nmark CLS, GCL, or SSaM axis. The drive activates the laser output at the start of the marking motion and deactivates the laser when the marking motion is complete. Marking motion is subject to all laser delays.

NOTE: For the laser output to activate, you must enable all the axes of your galvo drive. Make sure to include axes that do not engage in marking motion.

In the example that follows, X and Y are GL4 or Nmark CLS, GCL, or SSaM axes and Z is a mechanical axis controlled by a separate controller.

Example

MINUTES
ABSOLUTE

' Set the dominant feedrate to 60 user units/min.
F 60                   
' The X and Y axes move at a coordinated speed of 60 with the Galvo laser on.
LINEAR X10.0 Y20.0			
' The X axis moves at a coordinated speed of 60 with the Galvo laser on.
LINEAR X30.0          
' The Z axis moves at a coordinated speed 60 with the Galvo laser off.
LINEAR Z100.0         
' The X and Z axes move at a coordinated speed of 60 with the Galvo laser on.
LINEAR X60.0 Z200.0

You can command marking motion without issuing a LINEAR, CW, CCW, or BEZIER command. Refer to GALVO LASEROVERRIDE commands for more information.

Non-Marking Motion

Non-Marking motion refers to moving the GL4 or Nmark CLS, GCL, or SSaM axes while the laser output of the drive is deactivated. Non-Marking motion is accomplished by issuing a RAPID command, sometimes referred to as a Jump move.

It is possible to command non-marking motion with a LINEAR, CW, CCW or BEZIER command by using the GALVO LaserOverride command.

High-Speed Motion

The A3200 is capable of generating trajectory points at 48 kHz. The Nmark CLS and SSaM interpolate each trajectory point to 96 kHz. The GL4 and the Nmark GCL interpolate each trajectory point to a maximum of 192 kHz. You must change the MotionUpdateRate parameter to generate trajectory points at the higher rate.

Compensating for Scanning Errors

When adjoining two successive marking moves, it might be necessary to compensate for the lag between the position command and the actual mirror positions by reducing the speed of the GL4 or Nmark CLS, GCL, or SSaM axes. The A3200 can detect these conditions and automatically reduce the commanded speed to achieve optimal marking. Refer to CoordinatedAccelLimit and CoordinatedCircularAccelLimit parameters for more information.
