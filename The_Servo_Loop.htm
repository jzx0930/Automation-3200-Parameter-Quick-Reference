The Servo Loop

The A3200 SMC executes the user program commands and converts these into real-time position, velocity, and acceleration commands. These are sent over the FireWire or HyperWire network at precise time intervals to the digital drives. The drives are then responsible for closing the digital current, velocity, and position loops.

Note: The servo loop is responsible for interpreting the feedback (the actual current, position, and velocity) and adjusting the motor torque in order to minimize the position and velocity errors, accurately tracking the commanded motion profile.

Error is defined as commanded position (or velocity) minus the actual position (or velocity).

The settings of various axis parameters adjust or tune the servo loop for optimum performance through Digital Scope.

The servo loop also executes other high priority routines requiring high-speed sampling, in addition to the servo loop. These actions are guaranteed to execute at regular intervals.
