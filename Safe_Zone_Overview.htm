Safe Zone Overview

Safe zones prevent motion either into n-dimensional hypercubes or out of n-dimensional hypercubes. You can have a maximum of 32 safe zones. Safe zones are zero-based (indexed 0 through 31). Safe zones are typically specified in 1, 2, or 3 dimensions, but you can specify a maximum of 32 dimensions per safe zone.

When motion approaches the boundary of an area that is not permitted, the motion decelerates smoothly and stops 1 count before it gets to the boundary.

Safe zones do not protect drive motion. Do not try to protect HOME, MOVEOUTLIM, MSET or OSCILLATE motion by using safe zones.

Table:     Safe Zone Commands

Safe Zone Command

Description

SAFEZONE ON/OFF

Turns safe zones on and off.

SAFEZONE TYPE

Specifies the type of the safe zone.

SAFEZONE SET

Sets the dimension for a safe zone.

SAFEZONE CLEAR

Removes a dimension from a safe zone.

Safe Zone Violations

When a safe zone is violated, the AXISSTATUS_MotionClamped bit turns on for every axis in the violated safe zone. The bit stays on until the axis is moved again. Refer to Axis Status for more information.

The DATAITEM_SafeZoneActiveMask data signal shows a mask of all safe zones that are currently on. The DATAITEM_SafeZoneViolationMask data signal shows a mask of all safe zones that are currently violated. Violations are true after the axes decelerate to zero speed, and before any of the axes are moved again. Refer to SYSTEMSTATUS Function for more information.

Deceleration Rates

When axes decelerate because of safe zone violations, the deceleration typically occurs at the rate specified by the AbortDecelRate. But, the LimitDecelDistance parameter can limit the deceleration distance. If you use LimitDecelDistance, then the deceleration rate can be higher than the rate that is specified by the AbortDecelRate parameter.

You can turn on, turn off, or change the dimensions of safe zones at any time. But, if you make changes while axes in that zone are moving, then the axis might not be able to decelerate at the rate specified by the AbortDecelRate parameter and the axis might violate the safe zone.

If deceleration occurs because of safe zone violations or because of software limit clamps, then the SAFEZONE ON/OFF command does not have an effect until all of the decelerations complete.

Note: To make sure that the software limits have the correct effect, you must set the AbortDecelRate parameter to a value higher than any acceleration or deceleration rates that are used for motion. These rates include the rates specified by the DefaultRampRate, DefaultCoordinatedRampRate, and DefaultDependentCoordinatedRampRate parameters, and the rates specified by the RAMP RATE and RAMP RATE DEPENDENT commands.

Ramp Types

If deceleration occurs because of safe zone violations, then the ramp type is linear. Refer to Acceleration and Deceleration Ramping Overview for more information.

Asynchronous Motion

If the axis that is decelerating because of safe zone violations is part of a multi-axis asynchronous move, then the other axes in the move are not effected.

Synchronous Motion

If the axis that is decelerating because of safe zone violations is part of a multi-axis synchronous move, then the motion on the other axes is aborted at the rate specified by the AbortDecelRate Parameter for that axis. If the axis that is decelerating is a slave to another axis (for example handwheel, gearing, and camming), then the master axis is aborted at the rate specified by the AbortDecelRate parameter of the master and the master-slave relationship is removed.

Gantries

For gantries, safe zone should be applied only to the gantry master. Do not set a safe zone for a gantry slave.
